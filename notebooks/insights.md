# Titanic Correlation & Relationship Insights

1. Passengers travelling alone had a lower survival rate than passengers travelling with family/companions (IsAlone=1: 0.30 vs IsAlone=0: 0.51).
2. Survival outcomes differ strongly by gender: female passengers show the highest survival rate (0.74) compared with male (0.19).
3. Ticket class is a major driver: 1st class survival is highest (0.63), while 3rd class is lowest (0.24).
4. Higher fare tiers generally show higher survival rates, suggesting that wealthier passengers had better access to resources and evacuation opportunities.
5. Younger passengers (especially children) typically show higher survival rates, consistent with priority rules during evacuation (women and children first).
